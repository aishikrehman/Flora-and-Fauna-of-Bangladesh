Family: Meliaceae Genus: Chisocheton Species: Chisocheton Dysoxylifolius Author: Kurz Synonyms: English Name: Description: An Evergreen Tree, All Parts Glabrous. Leaves Large, Abruptly Pinnate, Leaflets Alternate, 22-30 Cm Long, Oblong Or Oblong-lanceolate, Somewhat Unequal, Acuminate At The Apex, Entire, Chartaceous, Glabrous, Petiolules Short, Puberulous. Flowers Almost Sessile, Tubular, Subtended By A Small, Lanceolate, Very Pubescent Bractlet And Forming Short Pubescent Racemes, Arranged In More Or Less Pubescent Panicles, Shorter Than The Leaves. Calyx Bell-shaped, Obsoletely 4-toothed, Minutely Pubescent. Petals About As Long As Calyx Lobes, Obovate-linear, Puberulous. Staminal Tube As Long As The Style, From The Middle Adnate To The Petals, Appressed Yellowish Pubescent Outside, 6-cleft At The Summit, The Lobes Oblong, Blunt, Glabrous, Anthers 6, Usually Attached Within The Tube, Alternating With The Lobes. Ovary And Styles Up To The Middle Appressed Pubescent. Fruit A Capsule, Opening Loculicidally, The Valves Bearing The Septum In The Middle. Seeds Obovoid To Spheroid, Cotyledons Collateral. Flowering Period: March-July Habit: Evergreen Forests.
